From the city of Issaquah, Washington, the American rock band Modest Mouse has been an indie giant since 1993. Known for musical idiosyncrasy and darkly comical lyrics, their style is both hard to describe and hard to compare. Their fourth album Good News for People Who Like Bad News helped the band achieve mainstream success, gaining a Grammy nomination for Best Alternative Music Album in 2004.

With lyrics sprawling existential subjects such as the afterlife, death, and one's place in the universe, lead singer Isaac Brock's bleating yet often abrasive vocals paired with the band's genre blending sound help create a unique, sometimes peculiar, experience.
